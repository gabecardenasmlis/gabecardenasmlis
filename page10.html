Accessibility Statement
I am committed to ensuring viewers can readily access the information on my website by complying with the World Wide Web Consortium’s Web Content Accesibility Guidelines 2.1. There are no audio visual components that require text alternatives and color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information. Text can be resized without assistive technology up to 200% without loss of content in both the HTML text of the website, as well as the downloadable PDF files. The PDF files (including the full downloadable portfolio) are stored on Google Drive and can be accessed through any current web browser or a PDF reader such as Adobe Acrobat. This website is accessible through the following browsers: Edge, Chrome, Firefox, and Safari through an Apple computer, iPhone, or iPad.The Marvel digital project link will take users to a different website hosted by Github that was created personally by me. For those that wish to not go to a different website, screenshots of the site are provided on the portfolio site and downloadable version of the portfolio. Unfortunately, the Gangsta Rap Libguide cannot be accessed online because it has not been published for the public by the UCLA Library. However, I did provide screenshots for the Libguide on the portfolio site as well. Visitors of my site are welcome to email me at gcardena@ucla.edu for any suggestions or feedback on any issues regarding access to the information on this site. This site was created using Mobirise and is hosted through Github. Mobirise was chosen for its “drop and drag” designer interface and for its menu option capabilities.
